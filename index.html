Academic Portfolio:

Samuel Alexander Bechtold

I will be graduating from Bridgewater State University in May of 2024 with a double-major in Photonic & Optical Engineering and Applied Physics. I also have a minor in Computer Engineering.
My research is in nonlinear and quantum optics. I am currently working on an Honors Thesis on All-Optical Switches utilizing Rogue "Freak" Waves with a modified nonlinear Schrödinger equation.
I have multiple collaborations with the Electronic Materials Group at Massachusetts Institute of Technology through MIT Nano's LEAP network partnership with BSU, Worchester Polytechnique Institute, and Stonehill college.
Other collaborative research projects include the study of Supercontinuum Generation and the design of Photonic Integrated Circuit waveguides that can support SCG working with Nippon Telegraph and
Telephone Japan, other projects include partnering with undergraduate and graduate students from Universidad Nacional de Colombia - Sede Medellín. The first being experiments on thin film wafers with the Z-Scan.
The second being research, simulation and testing of designs for an efficient entangled photon source on a PIC. Both projects resulted in poster presentations at SPIE Optics + Photonics 2022 and SPIE Photonics West 2023.
I've also worked in collaboration with vpiPhotonics and faculty from Institut d'Optique Paris-Saclay et Bordeaux on Heterodyne detection experiments for the characterization of materials through the utilization of Acousto-Optic Modeulation.

About Me

My family name is Sämu. I am a Swiss-American who was born and raised on Cape Cod, Massachusetts. I am a returning student after graduating with a bachelors degree in English from the University of Massachusetts at Amherst. I spent almost a decade working as a Cook, and eventually at a Sous Chef and Manager on Cape Cod and also spent some time in Austin Texas where I also worked in the service industry. I've also spent a few years working for Barnstable Public Schools as a Personal Care Assistant and Teacher Assistant. Due my change in careers upon re-entering the world of academics, I have a wide range of skills--both social and professional. I am currently working for Spark Photonics as a Technologist and spend my free time doing political organizing in my community for Environmental and Social Justice issues, having multiple years experience serving on the executive committee for Sierra Club Cape Cod and other progressive organizations.

Optica Student Chapter -

The mission of BEAM is to provide a safe and engaging environment for students at Bridgewater State University, who have a passion for working with light and optical devices and systems, to engage with one another and develop a

deeper appreciation for the numerous applications of light. In doing so, we will work to help develop student's academic, professional networks and working knowledge through the organization of educational and social events.

During the Spring 2023 semester the current BEAM President and I (currently the retired BEAM President) organized & facilitated the first ever student run conference for the Physics, Photonics & Optical Engineering Department titled, Harnessing Light: Optics and Photonics 2023.
